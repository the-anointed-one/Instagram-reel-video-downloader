When you save a TikTok video using the built-in Save Video button, TikTok automatically adds a watermark — your username and the TikTok logo — to the bottom of the video.

This is intentional. TikTok uses watermarks to track where content spreads and to drive new users back to the platform.

The problem? If you want to repost the video on Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, or share it in a presentation, the watermark looks unprofessional and clutters the video.
